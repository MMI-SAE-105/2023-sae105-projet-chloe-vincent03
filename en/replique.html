"He's at least... -8000" - Cult lines that made the film a hit

The film Asterix and Obelix: Mission Cleopatra , directed by Alain Chabat , is packed with memorable lines that have left their mark on cinema history. These lines, although added to the original storyline of the comic strip, contributed to the undeniable success of the film. One of the most famous is: "What kind of country is this? It can't be. It's at least... -800 !"

Jamel Debbouze

Humor and Originality through Unforgettable Replicas

The film is an adaptation of the original comic strip, but it has managed to inject its own touch of humour and originality with some unforgettable lines. In addition to the above quote, the film includes other gems such as "And I say thank you to life, I sing life, I dance life. I'm all about love" and "It's too quiet... I don't really like that... I prefer it when it's a little too quiet...". One of the most famous remains "But you know, I don't believe that there are good or bad situations."

pharaoh and pyramid of Egypt

The Unforgettable Scene That Gave Birth to the Iconic Replica

The line "What kind of country is this? It can't be. It's at least... -800" appears in a scene in the film where Edifis, the architect chosen by the Queen of Egypt to build her palace, travels to Gaul to meet up with an old friend. This friend is a wise druid who has the recipe for a magic potion that could save him from imprisonment or death. However, Edifis is caught unawares by the Gallic cold, moving from the Egyptian desert to an icy winter and lost in a snowy landscape. That's when he complains about the heat, leading to this memorable line.

Egyptian pyramid

Actor Jamel Debbouze and the Impact of the Replica in the Film

Actor Jamel Debbouze, who plays Edifis, brings his charisma and comic talent to this scene, transporting the audience into the story and making them laugh out loud. This line has become emblematic over time and can be quoted without context, being instantly recognisable to most viewers. It is a testament to the unique humour and lasting impact of Asterix and Obelix: Mission Cleopatra.
